In **machine learning**, **classification** is a type of **supervised learning** where the goal is to predict a **categorical label** (class) for given input data.

***

### ‚úÖ **What is Classification?**

*   **Definition:** Assigning items into predefined categories based on their features.
*   **Output:** Discrete values (e.g., ‚ÄúSpam‚Äù or ‚ÄúNot Spam‚Äù, ‚ÄúDisease‚Äù or ‚ÄúNo Disease‚Äù).
*   **Training:** Uses labeled data (features + correct class).

***

### **Examples of Classification Problems**

*   Email filtering: Spam vs. Not Spam
*   Sentiment analysis: Positive, Negative, Neutral
*   Medical diagnosis: Disease type prediction
*   Image recognition: Cat vs. Dog vs. Other

***

### **Types of Classification**

1.  **Binary Classification**
    *   Two classes (e.g., Yes/No, True/False)
2.  **Multi-Class Classification**
    *   More than two classes (e.g., classifying animals into species)
3.  **Multi-Label Classification**
    *   Each instance can belong to multiple classes (e.g., tagging an image with ‚Äúbeach‚Äù and ‚Äúsunset‚Äù)

***

### **Popular Classification Algorithms**

*   **Logistic Regression**
*   **Decision Trees**
*   **Random Forest**
*   **Support Vector Machines (SVM)**
*   **k-Nearest Neighbors (k-NN)**
*   **Naive Bayes**
*   **Neural Networks (MLP, CNN for images)**

***

### **Evaluation Metrics**

*   **Accuracy**
*   **Precision, Recall, F1-score**
*   **Confusion Matrix**
*   **ROC Curve & AUC**
